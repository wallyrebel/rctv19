In front of a packed gymnasium of cheering fans, the Ripley High School Tigers basketball team captured their third consecutive regional championship Friday night with a nail-biting 72-68 victory over the Blue Mountain Eagles.

## Game Highlights

The game lived up to its billing as the marquee matchup of the tournament. Both teams traded leads throughout the contest, with neither able to pull ahead by more than six points.

Senior point guard Marcus Williams led the Tigers with 24 points, including two crucial free throws in the final 30 seconds that sealed the victory.

"Our team showed incredible heart tonight," said Head Coach Robert Davis. "These young men have worked so hard all season, and they deserve this championship."

## Path to Victory

The Tigers' journey to the title included:

- **Quarterfinals**: Defeated Holly Springs 68-52
- **Semifinals**: Edged past New Albany 61-59 in overtime
- **Finals**: Topped Blue Mountain 72-68

## Looking Ahead

With the regional championship secured, the Tigers advance to the state tournament in Jackson next month. This marks the school's fifth state tournament appearance in the past decade.

The community is organizing bus trips to Jackson for fans who want to cheer on the team. Details will be announced next week.

## Player Recognition

Tournament MVP Marcus Williams credits his teammates for the success. "We play as a family," Williams said. "Everyone contributed, from the starters to the guys coming off the bench giving us energy."

Junior forward DeShawn Thompson added 18 points and 12 rebounds, while senior guard Tyler Johnson contributed 14 points and 6 assists.

*Congratulations to the Tigers and the entire Ripley community!*
