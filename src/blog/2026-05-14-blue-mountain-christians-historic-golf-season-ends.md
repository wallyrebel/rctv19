# Blue Mountain Christian Golf Season Concludes at NAIA Championship

Blue Mountain Christian University's men's golf program wrapped up what has been a historic season at the NAIA National Championship, though the team fell narrowly short of advancing past the initial rounds.

The 12th-ranked Eagles demonstrated their strength throughout the season but were unable to secure a spot in the team competition following two rounds of play at the national tournament. Despite the team's elimination, the program had reason to celebrate as sophomore Cobey Riddle earned individual advancement, continuing his participation in the championship.

Riddle's qualification as an individual competitor represents a significant achievement for the Blue Mountain Christian program, highlighting the talent that has contributed to what athletic officials are describing as a historic campaign for the Eagles.

The strong finish to the season reflects the program's progress and competitive standing within NAIA golf, positioning the team favorably for future national tournament appearances. Riddle's individual advancement provides a glimmer of continued success as the Eagles' season extends through the national championship competition.

Blue Mountain Christian's golf program has established itself as a competitive force in NAIA athletics, and this season's performance at the national level underscores the program's upward trajectory and potential for sustained excellence in the years ahead.
