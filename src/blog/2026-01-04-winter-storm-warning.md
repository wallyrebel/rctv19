The National Weather Service has issued a winter storm warning for Tippah County effective from Monday evening through Wednesday morning. Residents should prepare for significant winter weather impacts.

## Expected Conditions

Meteorologists are forecasting:

- **Snow accumulation**: 4-6 inches expected
- **Ice**: Quarter inch of ice possible before snow begins
- **Wind**: Gusts up to 25 mph creating some drifting
- **Temperatures**: Dropping to single digits Monday night

## School and Business Closings

Several closings and delays have already been announced:

- **Ripley Public Schools**: Closed Monday and Tuesday
- **Tippah County Schools**: Closed Monday and Tuesday
- **City Offices**: Opening at 10 AM Monday
- **Tippah County Library**: Closed Monday

Check RCTV19 for the latest closing announcements throughout the event.

## Safety Tips

Emergency Management Director Tom Reynolds offers these recommendations:

1. **Stay home** if possible during the worst of the storm
2. **Stock up** on essentials including food, water, and medications
3. **Check on neighbors**, especially elderly residents who may need assistance
4. **Keep phones charged** in case of power outages
5. **Never use generators indoors** due to carbon monoxide risk

## Road Conditions

The Mississippi Department of Transportation will have crews pre-treating highways Sunday evening. However, officials warn that road conditions may deteriorate quickly once precipitation begins.

> "If you don't have to travel, please stay home," said Highway Patrol spokesman Lt. James Carter. "We want everyone to stay safe."

## Warming Shelters

The following locations will serve as warming shelters if needed:

- First Baptist Church of Ripley - 201 Main Street
- Ripley Community Center - 450 City Park Road
- Tippah County Fairgrounds - Highway 4 East
